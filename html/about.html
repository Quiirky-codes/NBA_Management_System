NBA

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is a professional basketball league in North America.The league was founded in New York City on June 6, 1946

The National Basketball Association was founded at the Commodore Hotel in New York. Maurice Podoloff was the league's first president, a title later changed to commissioner. Eleven teams were part of that league, originally called the Basketball Association of America. Podoloff's name is now emblazoned on the NBA MVP trophy.

The NBA's list of all-time Legends continues to grow with each new generation, boasting names like LeBron James, Michael Jordan, Bill Russell, Stephen Curry, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Wilt Chamberlain, Kobe Bryant, Magic Johnson, Larry Bird, Kevin Durant, Oscar Robertson, John Havlicek, Tim Duncan, Rick Barry, John Stockton, Allen Iverson, Karl Malone, Elgin Baylor, David Robinson, Julius Erving, Charles Barkley and many more.
